Accreditation

1

WHO-GMP

The World Health Organization (WHO) is a specialized United Nations agency responsible for international public health, coordinating health-related activities and providing guidance.

2

GLP

GLP (Good Laboratory Practice) are guidelines assuring reliability of non-clinical research by maintaining quality, integrity, and consistency in laboratory studies for regulatory purposes.

3

GMP

GMP stands for Good Manufacturing Practice. It's a quality assurance system ensuring consistent and controlled production in industries like pharmaceuticals, food, and cosmetics, meeting regulatory standards.

4

ISO

ISO stands for International Organization for Standardization. It develops and publishes international standards that ensure products, services, and systems are safe, efficient, and of good quality.
